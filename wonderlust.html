Nikoko · In response to Wonder Lust

Vera Karlsson musical response to WONDER LUST by Atiéna R. Kilfa. An analysis that was developed in response to the work WONDER LUST by Atiéna R. Kilfa. After a summery of cinematic, musical and operatic influences from R. Kilfa directly from or influenced by the Romantic period. In the research I became interested in how opera and classical music from the romantic period broke with the harmonic composition from the Baroque period and Classicism with developing sounds of ‘dissonance’, representing nature and the emotional in mankind. I came to understand the massive influence this may have had on especially film score and music. For me it was also interesting to think of this influence in noise music. Another important finding for me was from R. Kilfa’s reference to the film Dunkirk with a soundtrack that excessively uses the Sheppard-Risset effect, an auditory illusion of endless build-up or fall in pitch. In the making of the mix many sounds from film music and classical scores were stretched, manipulated, layered and repeated.
